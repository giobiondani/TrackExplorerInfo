Elevation profiles

Display the elevation profiles of one or more tracks in the same chart
Select the values for the X axis (distance or time) and for the Y axis (elevation, cumulative elevation gain, distance or time); select the scale of the Y axis (starting from 0, from the lowest point, or normalizing all tracks based on their starting point); set the colors, backgrouns, gridlines as you like.

Examples
On the left, eight hikes are shown based on their actual elevation, with the scale starting at 0. This shows the tracks axactly where they are in elevation in reality: Mount Evans 2018 is at the top. Note the legend at the top associating each track to a color.
In the middle, the scale has been changed to relative - the starting points of all hikes are at the same level. This highlights the elevation gains: Pizzo Porcellizzo towers over the others.
On the rightm the plotted value is not the elevation, but the cumulative elevation gain. This highlights the tracks with the most sustained elevation gain: Cupid & Grizzly becomes more noticeable now.

View and edit your tracks' GPX data

From top left:

  • View all points in the GPX file, with date/time, coordinates, elevation, distance, elevation gain and time between points.
  • Trim the beginning or end of the track, if you started or stopped your device at the wrong time.
  • Combine multiple tracks - in this example, we are blending two tracks from different devices for the same activity, and you can see the results on the map and elevation profile. It's also possible to append tracks after each other.
  • Adjust the elevations in the track, if the data is too noisy or jittery. There are seven algorithms available, pick the one that works best for your situation and set the parameters as you wish.
  • Delete ski lift segments if you want a cleaner track for skiing. You can identify ski lifts by elevation gain or distance.
  • View the GPX file history - all edits performed by TrackExplorer, including all related parameters.
Also, delete time outliers (not shown) if there are long pauses in the track.
